      <h2 id="tribute-info">Here is the timeline of Shree  Atal Bihari Vajpai ji</h2>
      
      
      <ul>  <li><strong>1924</strong>  - Born: December 25, 1924, in Gwalior, British India.
</li>
        <li><strong>1942</strong> - Quit India Movement: Joined the Indian freedom struggle as part of the Quit India Movement against British rule.
</li>
        <li><strong>1951</strong> - Formation of Bharatiya Jana Sangh: Became a founding member of the Bharatiya Jana Sangh (BJS), the predecessor to the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP).
</li>
        <li><strong>1957</strong> - First Elected to Parliament: Elected as a Member of Parliament (MP) for the first time from Balrampur, Uttar Pradesh.
</li>
        <li><strong>1975</strong> - Emergency Period: Strongly opposed Indira Gandhi's Emergency, leading to his arrest and imprisonment.
</li>
        <li><strong>1977</strong> - Foreign Minister: Served as the Minister of External Affairs in the Janata Party government, earning international recognition for his diplomatic skills.
</li>
        <li><strong>1980</strong> - Formation of BJP: Co-founded the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) after the dissolution of the Janata Party, becoming its first president.
</li>
        <li><strong>1996</strong> - First Term as Prime Minister: Sworn in as the Prime Minister of India for the first time, although his government lasted only 13 days.
</li>
        <li><strong>1998</strong> - Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests: Successfully conducted nuclear tests in Pokhran, establishing India as a nuclear power.</li>
        <li><strong>1999</strong> - Kargil War: Led India during the Kargil conflict with Pakistan, displaying strong leadership and earning widespread respect.</li>
        <li><strong>2000</strong> - Golden Quadrilateral Project: Launched the ambitious Golden Quadrilateral highway project, improving infrastructure across India.
</li>
        <li><strong>2004</strong> - Retirement from Politics: Stepped down as Prime Minister after the BJP's defeat in the general elections and gradually withdrew from active politics.
2015</li>
        <li><strong>2015</strong> - Bharat Ratna Award: Conferred with India's highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna, in recognition of his contributions to the nation.</li>
        <li><strong>2018</strong> - August 16, 2018, in New Delhi, India. His passing marked the end of an era in Indian politics.</li>
</ul>
